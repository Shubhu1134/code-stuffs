= History of [[Quilling]] (Art Form) =

== History of [[Quilling]] (Art Form) - ==
'''Quilling''', also known as '''paper filigree''', is an art form that involves rolling, shaping, and gluing thin strips of paper to create decorative designs. The technique dates back centuries and has been practiced across various cultures worldwide.

=== Origins - ===
Quilling is believed to have originated during the Renaissance period in Europe. Nuns and monks used quilling to decorate religious objects, such as book covers and altarpieces, as an alternative to costly metal filigree. They often used gilded paper strips from books to mimic gold and silver.

=== Spread and Popularity - ===
During the 18th century, quilling gained popularity among European ladies as a pastime, particularly in England. It was taught as part of young women's education, alongside embroidery and painting. The craft spread to the Americas with colonists, where it became a cherished decorative art form.

=== Modern Revival - ===
In the late 20th century, quilling experienced a revival as a hobby and professional art. Artists began experimenting with contemporary designs, incorporating quilling into greeting cards, wall art, and even jewelry. Organizations like the North American Quilling Guild (NAQG) and Quilling Guild in the UK have played a significant role in promoting the art form globally.

=== Tools and Techniques - ===
Quilling requires basic tools such as a quilling needle, slotted tool, and tweezers. Techniques include rolling paper into coils, pinching them into shapes, and arranging them into intricate patterns. Common designs include floral motifs, scrollwork, and abstract shapes.

=== References - ===

* "A Short History of Quilling," The Quilling Guild. <nowiki>https://www.quilling-guild.co.uk</nowiki>
* Smyth, Mary. ''The Art of Quilling''. London: Artisan Press, 2015.
* "The Revival of Paper Crafts," ''Crafts Journal'', Vol. 22, 2018.
